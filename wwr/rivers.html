The Salmon River

The Salmon River winds its way deep within the Candid Church Wilderness. The people who lived next to this body of water gave it the name "The River of No Return", because so many people fell in love with its rugged beauty that has stood as testament for thousands of years.

Bears and bighorn sheep are frequently spotted on the hillsides surrounding the river, where herds of elk and deer graze. Ponderosa pines, which tower over the sandy campsites, provide shade from the sun or rain while in harmony with the river corridor.

The rapids are an adventure to experience!The Salmon is one of the longest rivers in the United States that has not been damaged. In the early summer, the water is higher, faster, and colder than in a free-flowing river. In the late summer, it is lower, slower, and warmer.There are good rapids throughout the summer, but families with children tend to prefer the slower and warmer water in the late summer.
